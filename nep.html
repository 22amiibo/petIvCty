Chemicals and Dangers

The toxic chemicals emitted by the facility pose severe health and environmental risks.

Formaldehyde

Formaldehyde is a carcinogen that can irritate the eyes and respiratory system, causing long-term health problems like cancer, especially for vulnerable groups.

Other Hazardous Chemicals

The facility releases volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and hazardous air pollutants, which can cause chronic illness, neurological damage, and air pollution.

Acute Risks

Accidents at the facility can lead to immediate health risks such as respiratory distress, skin irritation, and even fatalities due to sudden chemical exposure.

How is any of this even legal?

The "grandfathered-in" status of the NEP facility means it is exempt from newer, stricter environmental regulations that have been implemented to protect public health. This status allows the facility to continue operating under outdated rules, despite the significant health risks it poses to nearby residents. Due to that very reason, the facility is exempt from meeting today's air quality standards, and therefore, the community living nearby is still in a vulnerable position from the hazardous emissions without being policed by the control mechanism. This kind of exemption has drawn the need for renewing regulations in order to guarantee the safety of such communities.
